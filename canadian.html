3D Packaging Hydration Mist

This real Canadian hydration mist was created in a design course at Humber College. We were told to pick a water brand of our choice and create a product out of that brand as an additional piece that includes water. I chose to create the 3-D packaging for a hydration mist. I started out by doing rough sketches of how I wanted to frame the box for a better idea. Then I had to do research and figure out what legally had to be printed on the packaging in Canada. This includes a barcode, text translated in French, Expiry, date, and additional features that include the sizing and measurements of the ounces in each product.

I made a redesign of the Real Canadian water brand logo. I feel as though this brand is very outdated, and a redesign would be very beneficial for this water company. Creating a hydration mist with this water company would be a very smart decision as the beauty industry has many consumers, and would engage in a larger audience. Starting off with the process of my work I started with rough sketches, brainstorming different shapes and sizes of the box, and also did additional research to see what components should be added to the box to make the product be held in place. I chose a design for the package that includes an interior flap that will hold the product in place. I feel as though the package I decided to go with, is a success as it checks all the boxes for what I had envisioned. Designing this rebrand is very beneficial for my current landscape as it made me learn a lot about packaging as it is something I am not familiar with. Since I am so used to digital forms that include RGB, and a certain level of resolution, I feel as though a print design projects are very beneficial as it made me learn to put the settings to CMYK and a different resolution.
