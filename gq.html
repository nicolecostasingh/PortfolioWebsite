GQ Magazine Redesign

This GQ magazine was designed for my previous editorial design course at Humber College. The objective of this assignment was to redesign the cover, profile feature, cover story and mini feature of a magazine of our choice. I kept in mind design strategies and principles such as grid layout, colour, typography and hierarchy in the process of designing this magazine. I am very happy with the end result of this piece, it was an enjoyable piece I worked on.

I had a lot of design strategies planned out before I began working on the gentlemen’s quarterly magazine. I initially created the mini feature using the design principles of grid, layout, colour, and typography. After completing the first mini-feature, I analyzed my work and identified my mistakes. The primary achievements of my work, in my opinion, are going through each line and ensuring that there are no widows or orphans. Even if the text is not being read, I believe a layout that looks like it belongs in a magazine is enhanced by the absence of widows and orphans. Many people, especially men, find satisfaction in reading gentlemen’s quarterly magazines, using well-known musicians like Travis Scott and A$AP Rocky. Since these faces are now well-known musicians and the music industry has a significant following, many customers find interest in them. The combination of their musical style and fashion was a major inspiration for this magazine’s design. For me, the magazine’s initial development was crucial since typography and grid layouts are two of its primary components. This was incredibly helpful to me because site design relies heavily on understanding type weights and complimentary typefaces, grid layout, and item alignment.
